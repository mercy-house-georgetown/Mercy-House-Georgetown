Memory care in Georgetown, TX

At Mercy House Georgetown, every resident is embraced as an individual with their own story, routines, and preferences. Our approach begins with getting to know each person and creating a care plan tailored to their unique physical, cognitive, and emotional needs. Whether a resident needs support with daily activities, enhanced memory care, or a balance of both, our team ensures the right level of assistance is always available.

Each resident enjoys a private room with a bathroom, individual climate control, and plenty of space for personal belongings. The goal is to make the transition as smooth as possible, creating a sense of familiarity and independence from day one. We keep our resident numbers low so that our high staff-to-resident ratio can be maintained, allowing us to respond quickly and compassionately whenever assistance is needed. Safety is also a top priority, with secure facilities and technology like Roam Alert to ensure well-being around the clock.



Daily Living That Nurtures the Body and Soul

Mercy House isn’t just a place to live—it’s a place to thrive. Each day is filled with opportunities for meaningful connection and joyful engagement. Residents enjoy home-cooked meals served in a family-style dining room, where shared stories and laughter are just as important as nutrition. Activities are tailored to individual interests and capabilities, helping residents stay mentally active, socially involved, and emotionally uplifted.

Outdoor spaces play a significant role in daily life at our Georgetown location. From shaded porches to beautifully landscaped courtyards, nature is always within reach. Whether enjoying a quiet morning outside or participating in a group activity under the pergola, our residents are encouraged to savor the simple pleasures that promote overall wellness. This balance of structure and freedom allows them to maintain a sense of autonomy while still receiving the consistent support they need.



A Legacy of Compassion Passed Through Generations

The story of Mercy House begins with a simple, yet powerful desire: to create something better. Our founders, inspired by their own family’s struggle to find compassionate long-term care, saw firsthand how traditional institutions often fell short of meeting both the emotional and medical needs of aging loved ones. That experience led to the creation of a new kind of care model—one that mirrors the love, attention, and respect we give to our family members.

Today, the children and grandchildren of those early caregivers continue the mission, ensuring that every resident is welcomed into a space that feels safe, nurturing, and deeply personal. We believe caregiving is more than a job—it’s a calling. That’s why our staff is chosen not just for their qualifications, but for their empathy, patience, and ability to connect with others on a human level. At Mercy House, it’s not unusual for staff and residents to form deep, lasting bonds that feel more like friendship than formal care.
